The Spreadsheet Problem

Spreadsheets don't fail loudly. They fail quietly.

At scale, manual sheets create planning overhead, low-quality routes, and accountability gaps—then you pay for it in close rate.

1) Planning steals hours

Owners rebuild routes instead of reviewing performance and coaching.

2) Routes get worse → close rate drops

Zig-zag days mean fewer quality touches. The math is brutal.

3) Accountability becomes optional

When updates are manual, reps can “look busy” without results.

4) Handoffs multiply overhead

Office ↔ rep coordination eats time and adds mistakes.

Block fixes the workflow so you can run routes like a system, not a weekly scramble.

Key terms (quick glossary)

Sales operations

The system behind the reps: routes, assignments, follow-ups, and visibility.

Territory radius

Territory = the radius / coverage area around a rep manager (not just a polygon on a map).

Route quality

How efficiently a rep moves through leads. Better routes increase touches per day.

Close rate

The percent of conversations that become customers. Route + follow-up discipline impacts it.

Block
